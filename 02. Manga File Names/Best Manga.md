Best Manga

Here's a list of some of the greatest manga ever made, with nudity-containing series filtered out. That means I've removed titles like Berserk, Vagabond, Gantz, and a few others that are often cited as all-time greats but contain significant nudity.

**Action / Adventure / Shonen**

**One Piece** (Eiichiro Oda) — The best-selling manga of all time. Luffy and his crew sail the Grand Line searching for the ultimate treasure. Incredible worldbuilding, emotional storytelling, and a sense of adventure that never lets up. Still ongoing after 25+ years.

**Fullmetal Alchemist** (Hiromu Arakawa) — Two brothers use alchemy to try to restore their bodies after a failed attempt to resurrect their mother. Tight plotting, no filler, and one of the most satisfying endings in manga. Often cited as the most consistently excellent manga ever.

**Naruto** (Masashi Kishimoto) — An outcast ninja works his way up to become leader of his village. The themes of loneliness, redemption, and bonds between people run deep, especially in Part 1.

**Dragon Ball** (Akira Toriyama) — The manga that essentially defined the modern shonen battle genre. Goku's journey from a wild kid to earth's greatest defender. Pure energy and invention.

**My Hero Academia** (Kohei Horikoshi) — A boy born without powers in a superhero society receives the greatest power of all. A love letter to both shonen manga and Western comics.

**Hunter x Hunter** (Yoshihiro Togashi) — Gon searches for his absent father in a world of professional hunters. Brilliantly subversive and unpredictable, with some of the most complex power systems and arcs in manga. The Chimera Ant arc is legendary.

**Demon Slayer** (Koyoharu Gotouge) — A boy becomes a demon slayer after his family is killed and his sister is turned into a demon. Short, focused, emotionally devastating, with stunning art.

**Jujutsu Kaisen** (Gege Akutami) — A high schooler swallows a cursed object and enters the world of sorcerers. Dark, fast-paced, with incredible fight choreography.

**Haikyuu!!** (Haruichi Furudate) — A short volleyball player aims for the top. The best sports manga of the modern era — makes volleyball feel as intense as any battle series.

**Slam Dunk** (Takehiko Inoue) — A delinquent joins a basketball team to impress a girl and discovers a genuine passion for the sport. Widely considered the greatest sports manga ever made.

**Spy × Family** (Tatsuya Endo) — A spy, an assassin, and a telepath form a fake family, each hiding their identity. Heartwarming, funny, and clever.

**Death Note** (Tsugumi Ohba / Takeshi Obata) — A genius student finds a notebook that kills anyone whose name is written in it, and uses it to create a "perfect world." A cat-and-mouse thriller between him and the detective L. No action, all mind games.

**Dark / Mature (without nudity)**

**Attack on Titan** (Hajime Isayama) — Humanity lives behind walls to protect itself from giant man-eating Titans. What starts as survival horror evolves into one of the most ambitious political and philosophical narratives in manga. Polarizing ending, but the journey is unforgettable.

**Vinland Saga** (Makoto Yukimura) — A young Viking warrior consumed by revenge gradually discovers the meaning of peace. Starts as brutal action, transforms into something deeply meditative and moving.

**Monster** (Naoki Urasawa) — A Japanese surgeon in Germany saves a child's life, only to discover years later the child has become a serial killer. A slow-burn masterpiece of suspense and moral complexity.

**20th Century Boys** (Naoki Urasawa) — A group of childhood friends discover that a cult leader is using their childhood fantasy stories as a blueprint for world domination. Gripping mystery thriller.

**Pluto** (Naoki Urasawa) — A reimagining of an Astro Boy story as a dark, philosophical murder mystery about robots and what it means to be human. Short and brilliant.

**Promised Neverland** (Kaiu Shirai / Posuka Demizu) — Children in an idyllic orphanage discover the horrifying truth about their existence. The first arc is one of the best thriller setups in manga (the later arcs are weaker but the beginning is extraordinary).

**Kingdom** (Yasuhisa Hara) — Set during China's Warring States period, a young orphan aims to become the greatest general. Massive battles, real historical strategy, and compelling character growth.

**Sci-Fi / Psychological**

**Steins;Gate** (manga adaptation) — A group of friends accidentally discover time travel, with devastating consequences. Mind-bending and emotionally gutting.

**Slice of Life / Other**

**Aria** (Kozue Amano) — A gondolier on a terraformed Mars explores a recreated Venice. One of the most beautiful and peaceful manga you'll ever read.

**Frieren: Beyond Journey's End** (Kanehito Yamada / Tsukasa Abe) — An elf mage reflects on the meaning of human life and connection after her adventuring companions die of old age. Quietly profound and beautifully drawn.

**Chainsaw Man** (Tatsuki Fujimoto) — A destitute boy merges with a chainsaw devil and becomes a devil hunter. Wild, unpredictable, darkly funny, and surprisingly emotional. Note: it has some crude humor and suggestive content but stays short of actual nudity.

This is by no means exhaustive, but it covers the titles that consistently appear at the top of "greatest ever" lists. If any of these catch your eye, I'm happy to tell you more about them.

Here are the best horror manga, again filtering out those with significant nudity (which removes titles like Berserk, Gantz, Tomie, and some of Suehiro Maruo's work):

**The Junji Ito Essentials**

Junji Ito is the undisputed king of horror manga. His work relies on body horror, existential dread, and imagery that burrows into your brain and stays there.

**Uzumaki** — A small town becomes cursed by spirals. The spiral phenomena escalate, leading to gruesome deaths, bizarre deformations, and madness. Widely considered his masterpiece and one of the greatest horror works in any medium. The way something as mundane as a spiral shape becomes the most terrifying thing imaginable is pure genius.

**Gyo** — Dead fish start walking on land using mechanical legs, and the smell is unbearable. It sounds absurd, and it is, but Ito makes it genuinely horrifying. A faster, more action-oriented read than Uzumaki.

**Fragments of Horror** / **Shiver** — Short story collections that showcase Ito's range. Perfect entry points if you want a taste before committing to a longer series.

**Psychological Horror**

**Monster** (Naoki Urasawa) — Already mentioned in the previous list, but it deserves to be here too. A surgeon saves a child who grows up to become a serial killer. Less gore, more slow-building dread and moral horror. One of the most acclaimed manga ever made.

**The Promised Neverland** (Kaiu Shirai / Posuka Demizu) — Children in a seemingly perfect orphanage discover they're being raised as livestock. The first arc is a masterclass in tension and psychological cat-and-mouse games.

**Homunculus** (Hideo Yamamoto) — A homeless man undergoes trepanation (a hole drilled in his skull) and gains the ability to see people's inner psychological truths as grotesque visual distortions. Deeply unsettling and philosophical.

**Tokyo Ghoul** (Sui Ishida) — Ken Kaneki was a young and innocent boy whose life was completely ripped apart. Having been turned into a ghoul, a mutant race that lives in the dark within Japan, Kaneki endures insurmountable suffering while trying to adapt to his new existence. A tragic story about identity, isolation, and what it means to be human — wrapped in genuinely disturbing body horror.

**Survival Horror**

**Dragon Head** (Minetaro Mochizuki) — A psychological survival manga exploring fear, madness, and societal breakdown. After a tunnel collapse traps students on a bullet train, a small group of survivors must navigate a devastated, apocalyptic Japan. Claustrophobic and deeply unsettling.

**I Am a Hero** (Kengo Hanazawa) — A struggling manga artist witnesses the start of a zombie apocalypse in Tokyo. Starts as a slow-burn character study of a deeply ordinary, somewhat pathetic man, then explodes into one of the most realistic and terrifying zombie stories ever told. Note: has some brief nudity in spots but far less than most seinen horror.

**Doubt** (Yoshiki Tonogai) — When a virtual murder mystery game called Doubt captures Japan's attention, players must uncover the wolf in rabbit's clothing among them. If they fail, the rabbits will be killed off one at a time. A locked-room thriller — short, tense, and brutal.

**Supernatural / Cosmic Horror**

**Gou Tanabe's Lovecraft Adaptations** — For readers exploring horror manga with literary depth, these Lovecraft adaptations belong high on any horror manga list. Tanabe adapts stories like "The Hound," "The Colour Out of Space," and "At the Mountains of Madness" into stunning, atmospheric manga. If you're a Lovecraft fan, these are essential.

**Parasyte** (Hitoshi Iwaaki) — Alien parasites take over human brains, but one ends up in a teenager's right hand instead. He must coexist with the creature while fighting others who've fully consumed their hosts. A classic that explores what separates humans from monsters.

**Hell's Paradise: Jigokuraku** (Yuji Kaku) — A condemned ninja is sent to a mysterious island to find the elixir of immortality. The island is beautiful and absolutely horrifying, filled with monstrous flora and creatures. Great action with genuine horror elements.

**Classic / Historical Horror**

**Barefoot Gen** (Keiji Nakazawa) — The true horror lies in the fact that it adapts real events following the bomb in Hiroshima. Gen watches as people's skin falls off their bodies, the effects of radiation poisoning — everything that comes with a massive bomb. Not supernatural horror, but arguably the most genuinely horrifying manga ever made because it actually happened.

**Drifting Classroom** (Kazuo Umezu) — An entire elementary school is transported to a barren wasteland. The children must survive without adults in a world that's actively trying to kill them. A pioneer of horror manga from the 1970s that still holds up.

Horror manga tends to be more comfortable with graphic content than other genres, so a few of these will still have disturbing imagery (gore, body horror, psychological distress) — just not sexual nudity. If you want recommendations tailored to a specific type of horror (psychological, supernatural, survival), I'm happy to narrow it down further.
